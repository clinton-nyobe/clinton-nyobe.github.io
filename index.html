I am Stephane Cliton Nyobe, a network engineer and researcher based in Île-de-France, France. I currently work as a NetOps Engineer at Dassault Systèmes, leading network automation projects across multi-vendor environments (Cisco, Fortinet, Arista, Zscaler), while pursuing an MSc in Computer Networks and Connected Objects at CNAM Paris (2025–present).

I am actively seeking a PhD position in a laboratory focused on Green IoT and Network Automation, aiming to contribute at the intersection of computer networking, electronics, Infrastructure as Code (IaC), and data science, with an eco-friendly approach that serves real communities.

My research roots trace back to an internship at INRIA Saclay (TRiBe team), where I've contributed to build a testbed for SIMBox fraud detection in 4G LTE networks. More recently I co-authored and presented a paper accepted at IEEE BlackSeaCom 2026 conference on cost-effective smart agriculture for smallholder farmers in Sub-Saharan Africa.

Research interests:

  • Green IoT & sustainable networking for resource-constrained environments
  • Network automation, NetDevOps & Infrastructure as Code
  • Cellular network analysis & fraud detection (SIMBox / signaling)
  • Smart agriculture & ICT for development in Sub-Saharan Africa
  • Data-driven network monitoring and observability
